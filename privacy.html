Ariel Hotel — Airport Parking

Privacy Policy — How we collect, use and protect your personal information

Last updated: April 2026  |  Effective: April 2026  |  ICO Registration: ZA000000

1. Who we are

This privacy policy applies to the airport parking management service operated at Ariel Hotel, Bath Road, Hayes, Middlesex, UB3 5AJ (referred to in this policy as "we", "us" or "the Hotel").

We are registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Our ICO registration number is shown above.

This policy describes how we collect, use and protect personal information when you use our airport parking service, including our online pre-registration form at heathrowariel.co.uk.

2. What personal information we collect

We collect only the information necessary to manage your parking booking and fulfil our obligations to you.

Information How it is collected Why we need it
Full name You provide it when booking To identify your booking and vehicle
Vehicle registration plate You provide it when booking To identify and locate your vehicle
Mobile phone number You provide it when booking To contact you regarding your booking if needed
Email address You provide it when booking To send booking confirmation, reminders and collection notices
Hotel room number You provide it when booking To link your parking to your hotel stay

We do not collect payment card details, passport or ID numbers, health information, or any sensitive personal data as part of the parking service.

3. Legal basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing your personal information. We rely on the following:

  • Contract performance — processing your name, registration plate, dates and contact details is necessary to fulfil the parking agreement you enter into with us.
  • Legitimate interests — vehicle photographs and movement logs are kept to protect both you and the Hotel in the event of a dispute about vehicle condition or location. We have a legitimate interest in maintaining the security and integrity of the parking operation.
  • Legal obligation — we may be required to retain certain records to comply with applicable law, including VAT and accounting obligations.
  • Consent — when you tick the agreement box on our booking form, you consent to us processing your information to manage your parking booking and send you booking-related emails.

4. How we use your information

We use your personal information only for the following purposes:

  • Processing and confirming your parking booking
  • Sending you a booking confirmation email
  • Sending you a reminder when your collection date approaches
  • Contacting you if your vehicle is overdue for collection
  • Managing the safe storage and movement of your vehicle
  • Resolving any disputes regarding vehicle condition or location
  • Maintaining records for accounting and legal compliance
We do not use your information for marketing purposes. We will not send you promotional emails, share your information with third parties for marketing, or add you to any mailing lists. Every email we send is directly related to your parking booking.

5. Who we share your information with

We share your information only where necessary:

Off-site parking contractors

If your vehicle is moved to an approved off-site storage facility, we share your vehicle registration plate and booking reference with that contractor. This is necessary to fulfil the parking agreement. Contractors are bound by confidentiality obligations and are not permitted to use your information for any other purpose.

Technology service providers

Our booking system is built on Supabase (database, hosted on servers within the European Union) and Resend (email delivery). Both providers process your data on our behalf under data processing agreements in accordance with UK GDPR. Your data is not sold or shared beyond what is necessary to operate the system.

Legal requirements

We may disclose your information to law enforcement or regulatory bodies if required to do so by law, court order, or to protect the safety of persons or property.

We do not share your information with any marketing companies, data brokers, or other third parties.

6. Where your data is stored and how long we keep it

Your data is stored securely in a cloud database hosted within the European Union. All data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS).

We retain your personal information for the following periods:

7. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct any inaccurate information.
  • Right to erasure — you can ask us to delete your personal data. We will action this within 30 days unless we are required by law to retain it (for example, for tax or accounting purposes).
  • Right to restrict processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability — you can ask for your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — you can object to our processing of your data where we rely on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 9. We will respond within 30 days. There is no charge for making a request.

8. How we protect your information

We take the security of your personal information seriously. Our measures include:

  • All data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS connections
  • Staff access to the booking system requires a verified email address and two-factor authentication
  • Role-based access controls limit which staff can view sensitive information
  • All staff actions are recorded in an audit log
  • Database access is restricted and monitored
  • Automatic data deletion after the retention periods above

In the unlikely event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and inform affected individuals without undue delay, as required by UK GDPR.
